Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about using 2000s Filter Lab and our retro photo filters.

What image formats are supported?
We support JPEG, PNG, BMP, GIF, TIFF, and WEBP formats. The maximum file size is 50MB and maximum resolution is 4K (4096x4096 pixels).
How do I use the filters?
Simply upload your image by dragging and dropping or clicking the upload area, select a filter from the categories, and click "Generate" to apply the effect to your photo.
Are my images stored on your servers?
No, we process all images in memory and don't store them on our servers. Your privacy is important to us, and your images are processed securely without being saved.
What makes these filters "2000s style"?
Our filters recreate the aesthetic of early 2000s photography, including CCD camera effects, VHS-style color grading, film grain, and the distinctive look of digital cameras from that era.
Can I use the filtered images commercially?
Yes, you own the rights to your original images and the filtered versions. You can use them for personal or commercial purposes as you see fit.
Why is my image taking a long time to process?
Processing time depends on image size and complexity. Larger images (close to 4K resolution) may take longer. If processing seems stuck, try refreshing the page and uploading again.
Do you offer mobile apps?
Currently, we only offer a web-based service that works on all devices through your browser. Our website is fully responsive and optimized for mobile use.
How can I get the best results?
For best results, use high-quality images with good lighting. The filters work well with portraits, landscapes, and everyday photos. Experiment with different filter categories to find your preferred style.
Is the service free to use?
Yes, 2000s Filter Lab is completely free to use. You can process as many images as you like without any cost or registration required.
I'm having technical issues. How can I get help?
If you're experiencing technical problems, please try refreshing the page first. If the issue persists, contact our support team through the Contact page, and we'll be happy to help you.
